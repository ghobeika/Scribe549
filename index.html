Scribe

Scribe will allow a lecturer to seemlessly record and create informative videos.
Our prototype will use an array of sensors to triangulate chalk on a board,
and stream a video of what is being written seemlessly after being hooked up to any blackboard.

created by Dillon Lareau, Jane Threefoot, Nitsan Shai and Gabe Hobeika

Learn More

Motivation

We seek to make any blackboard surface 'smart' allowing a clear recording and transmission of
the text on a blackboard to a videoscreen or computer. Smartboards are sometimes used today but they have high entry and setup cost. Video cameras area also used but they are imperfect, and professors block text by standing infront of the board.

Competitor 1: Smartboards

Smartboards are related to our project as they also seek to revolutionize the 'board' in a classroom. Our produt provides less functionality than a smartboard, bugt should be cheaper, and should be able to be installed anywhere, as well as provide our unique video stream featureset.

Competitor 2: Cameras

Cameras provide similar functionality to our product. We aim to change the way lectures are recorded by giving students an uninhibited view of whats being written on boards, and a clearer focus on the boards in order to provide a more accurate record of lecture.

Competitor 3: Tablets and Projectorst

Again, this provides a similar functionality to our product, but storing live lectures as videos and recording them is unintuitive. Also, tablets don't provide the 'comfort' of lecturing on a blackboard, we seek to be as unintrusive in the classroom as possible.

Competitor 4: Kahn Academy

At the end of the day, our products videos will turn out a good deal like a Khan academy video. The main difference is rather than dedicate time alone with a computer to create a video lecture, our product will allow professors do this on the fly by creating the same style product during a live lecture.

Requirements

What we would like scribe to accomplish in the end

  • Scribe Shall

    Accurately track writing on a blackboard by one user
    Accurately locate start and stop points of strokes
    Track path between those two points reliably
    Accurately generate an animation from sensor data
    Accurately sync the audio stream with the generated animation
    Have the ability for any battery powered components to last at least one lecture per charge
    Be able to tell the difference between chalk and erasers touching the board
    Be easy for lecturers to install and use
    Have final results accessible via internet, not just stored locally

  • Scribe Shall Not

    Fall off the board while in use, or become disconnected due to board sliding.
    Be obtrusive and get in the way of a lecturer
    Shut off at any time during a lecture

  • Scribe Should

    Have a user-friendly and secure web app to access the video(s)
    Be able to do this tracking/recording fast enough to keep up with a lecture in real time (for livestreaming purposes)
    Be able to be set up on an arbitrary board
    Be minimally invasive to the classroom
    Have a reasonable solution if the connection is dropped briefly during use
    Have final results viewable by any students registered to a course

  • Scribe Should Not

    Be ‘laggy’ or annoying to use
    Be less convenient than sitting in a classroom to watch a lecture
    Be time intensive to use, taxing on students or instructors
    Cost over $400 to reproduce for use in a classroom setting

What we might be using.

Hardware

The Sensors we may be using, and how we plan to connect them

We will likely be using microphone and piezo sensors to triangulate chalk position. These sensors can be placed around the chalkboard, and based on the readings from them we should be able to get a sense of whater a touch is occuring on the board.

Additionally we will be using A2D chips in order to convert the analog data from these sensors into digital signals that we can then perform analysis on. A2D chips are relatively cheap, and reliable, so we will likely be looking for the best value here when picking our an A2D

As far as an embedded processor goes, we estimate that we need to handle about 100,000 samples per second and also package them and forward the data. Smaller processors would be pushing a limit here, so we are looking towards using an Intel Edison, which members of our team already have experience using. We need basic I/O and general computing here

Software and Webtech

How we'll transmit the data, package it, and create videos

TCP Sockets will be used as they are standard and easy to set up in any language, also if packets are dropped, there are built in methods to resent data

Our server will be written using Django, as a majority of our team is familiar with that framework. In addition to that we will be able to quickly write python applications for testing certain parts of our code, such as live animation drawing. These apps should also be easy to translate for use on the webserver if we use Django to service the app. Django's built in security features will also make making a final webapp with user authentication easier.

Our data processing code is what will be responsible for converting raw data into usable data. The outputs of our data processing should be series of (x,y) coordinates, a label that tells what is otuching the board, and the time that that touch is happening.

We will also look into multiple libraries in python to "draw" from coordinates. So far MatPlotlib, and the Turtle Tkinter libraries seem like they may be viable options. To add audio to our lectures we may use moviepy or ffmpeg. We will be analysing which library provides the most favorable results with out product before we pick one.
